# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Kairo is built first for Abu: a focused learner using a local review tool to practice recall, get precise model feedback, and decide memory confidence without being gamified or patronized.

## Product Purpose

Kairo Review helps a learner import CSV cards, answer one card at a time, receive sharp LLM coaching, and self-rate for spaced repetition. Success means faster, clearer review sessions where the learner sees the fragile point, corrects it, and moves on.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, quiet, exacting. The product should feel like an expert review instrument: fast, focused, and serious without becoming cold or sterile.

## Anti-references

Avoid Duolingo-style gamification, mascot energy, dopamine loops, confetti, childish rewards, and generic SaaS polish. Avoid bloated study dashboards that make review feel like administration.

## Design Principles

1. Preserve review flow: every layout decision should reduce interruption between question, answer, feedback, and rating.
2. Expert over entertainer: use precise language and restrained feedback, not cheerleading.
3. Make the next action obvious: the learner should never hunt for how to continue.
4. Compress without cramping: dense enough for speed, spacious enough for thinking.
5. Respect learner judgment: coaching informs, but rating remains clearly owned by the learner.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use good accessibility defaults: semantic structure, visible focus states, sufficient contrast, keyboard shortcuts that do not trap text input, reduced-motion-safe interactions, and layouts that remain usable on narrow screens.
